Your Field Operations Quickstart

Welcome to the RCSO Bot operations guide! Using the bot is designed to be super easy, letting you focus on the action. Think of this as your quick training manual for core daily tasks, no complex jargon, just smooth operations.

I. Clocking In and Out (10-8 and 10-7)

This is the most important step! Starting and ending your shift correctly ensures your time is logged for payroll and promotion points.

  1. Begin Your Shift (10-8)

    To start your duty, type the command /shift_panel into any channel. The bot will present an interactive panel. Click the START SHIFT (10-8) button. The system automatically gives you the On Duty role, starts your timer, and creates a private log thread for your reference.

  2. Update Your Status During Duty

    If you need to log a major event or take a break, use the command /status_update followed by a short note (for example, /status_update 10-6 break for lunch). This action records the time and reason in your private shift log thread, keeping your record clean.

  3. Ending Your Shift (10-7)

    When your shift is complete, use the /shift_panel command again. Click the END SHIFT (10-7) button. The system stops your timer, removes your On Duty role, calculates your total time and promotion points earned, and locks your shift log thread.

II. Submitting Official Reports

The bot makes filing reports easy using pop up forms (modals) instead of complicated text entries.

  1. Access the Reporting Hub

    To begin any report, simply type /report_panel. This displays an interactive panel with buttons for all report types: Arrest/Booking, Citation, or Incident Report.

  2. Filling Out the Modal Form

    Select the report type (for example, Arrest). A clean, separate pop up window will appear on your screen. Enter all required details, such as suspect name, vehicle, charges, and the narrative. Use clear, objective language in your description.

  3. Submission and Supervisor Review

    Once submitted, the report is automatically logged in the RECORDS channel and a thread is opened for supervisor review. No further action is required from you unless a supervisor contacts you to request a correction or resubmission.

III. Training and FTO Status

If you are a Trainee, the bot keeps track of your hours and records your progress.

  1. Starting a Training Session

    You do not start the timer! The FTO (Field Training Officer) or Instructor uses the /training_panel to begin the session. Simply join the dedicated TRAINING_VC voice channel when instructed. The bot automatically logs your active time, pausing if you mute or deafen.

  2. Checking Your Progress

    You can view your overall training records and hours logged by using the command /view_cadet_profile [Your Name]. This is helpful for monitoring your progress toward certification.

IV. Pro Tips and Helpful Hints

Tip: Command Shortcuts

Do not type the full command! Once you type the slash /, a menu pops up. Just start typing the name (for example, "shif" for /shift_panel) and select it from the list. It is much faster than typing everything out.

Tip: Keep Your Status Fresh

When using /status_update, be specific. Instead of just "10-6," try "10-6 vehicle maintenance." Detailed logs look professional and help command staff understand your activity at a glance.

Tip: When in Doubt, Check the Manual

If you are ever unsure about a command or a procedure, the full /commands list has descriptions of every single function, even the administrative ones. You have just joined an exclusive club of capable, confident pros!
